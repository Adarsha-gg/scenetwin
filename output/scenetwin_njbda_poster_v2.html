How SceneTwin Scores AD

The pipeline checks both grounding and comprehension. CLIP catches wrong-scene text; ADQA catches vague text that looks plausible but does not answer visual questions.

Video evidenceSample frames directly from the clip, not from the professional AD.
→
Two judgesCLIP-L14 grounding plus frame-grounded question answering.
→
Clip-local rankNormalize within clip, then rank four candidate descriptions.
CLIP-L14 Measures whether description text matches sampled video frames. Strong against hallucinated or wrong-scene AD.
Frame-Grounded ADQA Generates visual comprehension questions from frames, then grades anonymized candidate ADs blind.

What We Tried That Failed

These branches matter because they narrow the honest claim. The survivor is reference-free scoring plus TRIBE triage.

Raw neural text scoringRich wrong descriptions can still look neurally plausible.
Description Gain / MVRRUnstable at small scale; not reliable enough for the headline.
ROI content typingGlasser/Destrieux proxy typing was near weak-prototype territory, not a final claim.
TRIBE need-weighted ADQAAdaptive weighting changed ρ by less than ~0.002 versus fixed 50/50.
Neural closurePA+AD to PAV remained hard to interpret; verbosity dominated language-network signal.
OCR-only coverageUseful as a feature, not sufficient as a general AD quality metric.

Takeaway: SceneTwin makes audio-description QA scalable by separating the problem into grounded visual evidence, listener-style comprehension, and upstream risk triage. The strongest claim is practical: good AD can be audited without a reference description.
